Latvian cities


Latvia has 7 cities and 81 towns.

The largest city of Latvia is Rīga, both in terms of population and size. You could make an argument that it's the center of the Baltics for a number of aspects like finance, culture, sport, industry etc.

Daugavpils

Daugavpils is a city in south eastern part of Latvia. It's the second largest city in Latvia and is 230km away from the capital. It is close to the Lithuanian and Belorussian border. The city has around 82k inhabitants and has a very large percentage of Russian speaking population

Liepāja

Located on the Baltic Sea in the North-Western cornet of Latvia. The city has an ice-free port. With a population of around 68k it ranks as 4th most populous city in Latvia. This city is known as the "Pilsēta, kurā piedzimst vējš", which means the city where the wind is born. The city name comes from Libau, which was the village established by Teutonic order here in 1263.

Now a little about Jelgava. Located southwest of Rīga, it is 41km away from the capital. It is the largest city in Zemgale. Jelgava was a city that was really badly devastated during world war 2, so some parts of the city have newer buildings and some parts of the city look very modern in result. Home to approximately 55k inhabitants, it is also a railway center. Jelgava is situated on the banks of the River Lielupe. It is known for its impressive architecture, including the Jelgava Palace, which was once the residence of the Dukes of Courland.

Jūrmala

Jūrmala is the largest resort city in Latvia. It is made of a few parts like- Sloka, Kauguri, Vaivari, Dubulti, Majori, Dzintari, Bulduri, Ķemeri, Priedaine etc.
