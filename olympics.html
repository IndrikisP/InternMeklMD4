2012 Summer Olympics

Host City: London, United Kingdom

The 2012 Summer Olympics, officially known as the Games of the XXX Olympiad, were held in London, United Kingdom. It was the third time that London had hosted the Olympic Games, previously hosting them in 1908 and 1948.

Dates: July 27 - August 12, 2012

The Summer Olympics lasted for 17 days, starting on July 27 and concluding on August 12, 2012. During this time, athletes from around the world competed in various sporting events.

Participating Nations: 204

A total of 204 nations participated in the 2012 Summer Olympics. This made it one of the largest international events, showcasing the diversity and talent of athletes from around the globe.

Key Venues:

  • Olympic Stadium - The main stadium of the Olympics, hosting the opening and closing ceremonies, as well as track and field events.
  • Aquatics Centre - Venue for swimming, diving, synchronized swimming, and water polo.
  • Velodrome - Indoor track cycling arena.
  • All England Lawn Tennis and Croquet Club - Venue for tennis events, including Wimbledon Championships.
  • ExCeL London - Exhibition and Conference Centre hosting various sports, such as boxing, fencing, judo, and table tennis.

Highlights:

The 2012 Summer Olympics witnessed several memorable moments, including:

  • Usain Bolt's record-breaking performances in the men's 100m and 200m sprints.
  • Michael Phelps becoming the most decorated Olympian of all time, winning a total of 22 medals.
  • Great Britain's success in cycling, winning a total of 12 medals, including 8 golds.
  • Gabby Douglas becoming the first African-American gymnast to win the individual all-around gold medal.
  • The "Super Saturday" when Great Britain won three gold medals in athletics in the span of an hour.
